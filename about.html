About

The maker, the shop, the approach

01 / The Maker

One Engineer.
Every Product.

Waypoint Machine Works is owned and operated by a mechanical and manufacturing engineer with roughly a decade of experience across aerospace, automotive, plastics, consumer electronics, and food and beverage industries.

Every product sold under the Waypoint family of brands is designed, programmed, machined, finished, and shipped by the same person. There is no design team, no outsourced production, and no dropshipping operation. This is a real person making real things in a real shop.

That direct line from engineer to finished product is the point. It means faster iteration, tighter quality control, and the ability to stand behind every piece that leaves the building.

02 / The Shop

30 × 40 × 14'

[ Shop interior — wide shot of the building, equipment layout ]

A metal building in Sanger, Texas — Denton County, about 50 miles north of Dallas. Purpose-built for CNC machining, fabrication, and fulfillment. Everything from raw bar stock to finished, packaged product happens under one roof.

The shop runs Haas CNC equipment for milling and turning, a fiber laser for cutting and engraving, and supporting equipment for finishing and post-processing. It's a compact operation built for flexibility and fast turnaround — not a production line.

03 / The Approach

How We Work

Quality over quantity. Every product is designed in-house and manufactured on-site. There are no vendor lead times to wait on, no overseas tooling to manage, and no middlemen between the engineer and the machine.

When something needs to change — a design improvement, a material upgrade, a new finish option — it happens in days, not months. That speed and control is what lets a small shop compete with companies ten times its size.

Made in Sanger, Texas. Made by the person who designed it.
